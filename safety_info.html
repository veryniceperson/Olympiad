How to keep yourself safe What to do if you think you are sick

The webpage will give you advice on how to stay safe from dangerous infections. This guide can be applied to most infectious diseases.

1. Washing your hands often

Washing your hands is one of the most important things you can do in order to keep yourself and others safe. Your hands are full of microscopic organisms, because you touch many contaminated things everyday. It's just a part of your life. Moreover, you touch your face, eyes, mouth, nose and ears with your hands. You wouldn't want to do that with hands that are contaminated with various diseases, because those parts of your body are the main entry points for disease. So wash your hands often, like after you visit the bathroom, before you eat or prepare, after having a trip outside and even after using your phone. Just make sure that you wash your hands before you touch any of the body's disease main entry points (mouth, eyes, nose and ears). If hand washing isn't avaliable you can use alcohol based disinfectants.

2. Preparing foods and drinks safely

Microscopic organisms such as germs quickly grow on food and if not handled/prepared properly, the person which consumes the food might contract an infection and suffer things like vomiting and diarrhea. You should keep surfaces clean (such as cutting boards), wash fruits and vegetables and cook food at appropriate temperatures. In addition, don't leave food out and keep it inside a refrigerator in order to maintan safety.

3. Cough or sneeze into your elbow

You should cough or sneeze into your elbow instead of your hands, because you are likely to touch something or someone else after you have sneezed and you have a high chance of infecting someone. On the other hand, whenever you cough/sneeze into your elbow you have a low chance of infecting someone, because this method effectively blocks all microscopic organisms that come out as you sneeze.

4. Don't share personal items

It is highly recommended that you don't personal objects such as toothbrushes, towels, razors and especially needles. It is very easy to get infected through these objects if they are contaminated and in order to stay safe, only use your personal objects. You should also remember that you should use needles only once and throw them away afterwards.

5. Make sure you clean commonly used surfaces

You should make sure that you wipe surface that are commonly used with a napkin and some kind of disinfectant regularly (once a week), especially in kitchens and bathrooms, because there are greater deals of microscopic organisms there. In addition you should clean your toilet and shower, bath and sink with some kind of disinfectant such as toilet cleaner solution and soap and water. This control the growth of microscopic organisms and slows their population growth, as bacteria and fungus grow at a faster rate in moist places. In addition, if someone in your home is ill, make sure you regulary disinfect surfaces that they come on contact with, so others don't get infected.

6. Do not visit someone if they are sick

Do not visit someone if they are sick, even while they aren't home, because you would have a very high chance of getting infected. Instead tell them to stay home and treat themselves using appropriate methods. If you have no choice, make sure you visit them while maintaining appropriate precautions such as wearing a mask, maintain a distance with them and washing yourself after visiting them, in order to reduce the chances of contracting their disease.

7. If you come in contact with a sick person, make sure to take these precautions

If you are a medical worker and you had come in contact with a person who has a communicable disease, make sure that you take the precautions listed below:

  • Make sure that you wear a mask
  • Make sure that you wear gloves
  • Wear a face sheild if necessary
  • Wear a protection suit if necessary
  • Clean yourself after a shift by showering or taking a bath
  • Don't come in physical contact with others after being with an infected patient, as you may infect them
  • Make sure that you regularly disinfect your hands with a disinfect
  • After you treat a infected patient, make sure to change your suit, mask, gloves, etc
  • Before you leave the facility, wash your hands and disinfect them

    • 8. Get vaccinated

    You should make sure that you get your required and recommended vaccines, because they are very effective and preventing the dangerous symptoms of a disease. There are required vaccines for both adults and children, which are designed to protect them from a vast amount of communicable diseases. In addition, there are recommended vaccines for regional diseases, such as the Hepatitis B for when you travel to Saudi Arabia.

    9. Avoiding contact with wild animals

    You should avoid contact with wild animals because they might be contaminated with parasites such as ticks and have a chance of carrying a communicable disease, such as rabies. Coming in contact with wild animals puts you and your pet at risk of infection of both microscopic organisms and parasites, why is why you must avoid it at all times.
